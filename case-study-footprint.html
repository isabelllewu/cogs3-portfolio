The Problem

Climate change is one of the most urgent issues of our time — yet most people feel powerless to act. Existing tools are either too complex, too guilt-driven, or fail to connect individual behavior to real-world impact.

"How might we help everyday people understand and reduce their personal carbon footprint in a way that feels motivating, not overwhelming?"

This project was completed as a class assignment with a brief to design a mobile app that promotes positive climate action through behavior change.

02

Project Goal

The project aimed to effectively communicate about climate change through different mediums that each student was passionate about.

03

Design Process

Working solo across the full design lifecycle — from early sketches to a polished, interactive prototype.

01

Define & Scope

Identified scope of app, needed to personalize the data to the user's lifestyle, mapped out core features that would be needed.

02

Competitive Audit

Analyzed 3 existing climate apps to identify UX failures, unmet needs, and design patterns worth adopting or avoiding.

03

Wireframes

Sketched low-fidelity flows for onboarding, the home dashboard, and the impact summary screen. Iterated on information hierarchy before moving to visual design.

04

Visual Design System

Moodboarded to collect design systems. Opted for high-contrast accents & footprint inspired leaves to represent the environmental theme of the brand.

05

High-Fidelity Prototype

Produced full UI screens for iOS and Android, including onboarding, home feed, habit log, progress tracking, and settings. Linked screens into an interactive Figma prototype.

04

Key Design Decisions

Each screen was designed to not make the user feel guilty, but to inform and make them feel like they are making an impact

🌱

Impact-First Home Screen

The dashboard leads with what you've saved this week — trees, emissions, miles — before showing what to do next. Positive reinforcement first.

📋

One-Tap Habit Logging

Reduced tracking friction to a single tap per habit. No forms, no calculations — the app handles the math behind the scenes.

📈

Weekly Progress View

A simple visual chart showing carbon impact over time, designed to reveal trends without overwhelming users with data.

🎯

Personalized Action Plans

Onboarding asks 3 lifestyle questions and generates a custom set of habits, making the app feel relevant from day one.

05

Reflection & Next Steps

As a solo concept project, this pushed me to make every design decision independently and explain my choices, as I had to with my professor.

What I learned

Designing to show collective activism requires a lot of data gathering.

What I'm proud of

The visual design system — dark, organic, and high-contrast — felt intentional and differentiated from the generic "eco green" look most climate apps default to.

What I'd do differently

I'd run usability testing with real users — even informal sessions — to validate that the one-tap logging actually reduces friction as intended.

If this were real

I'd explore a social layer — sharing streaks, group challenges — since research shows community accountability significantly improves habit retention.
